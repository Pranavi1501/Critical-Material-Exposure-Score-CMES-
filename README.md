# Critical Material Exposure Score (CMES)

**A design-stage decision-support tool for assessing critical mineral dependency 
in clean-energy building systems.**
---
## What it does

CMES quantifies a building's exposure to critical and conflict mineral supply chain 
risk — before procurement decisions are locked in.

Input your building's clean-energy systems and receive:
- A **0–100 risk score** across 6 weighted dimensions
- A **material-level breakdown** showing kg of each critical mineral embedded
- **Actionable design recommendations** to reduce exposure without compromising performance

## Systems covered

| Technology | Options |
|---|---|
| Solar PV | Mono-Si, Low-silver Mono-Si, Poly-Si, Thin-film CdTe |
| Battery storage | NMC 622, NMC 811, LFP |
| Heat pump | Air source (standard), Air source (rare-earth free), Ground source |
| EV charging | AC 7kW, AC 22kW, DC 50kW |
| Wiring | Copper (4mm², 6mm²), Aluminium |

## Scoring dimensions

| Dimension | Weight | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| Geopolitical concentration | 25% | Highest policy priority — China/DRC dominance |
| Supply-demand trajectory | 20% | Projected deficits to 2030 |
| Recyclability | 20% | Circular economy alignment |
| Embodied carbon | 15% | LCA methodology |
| Substitution potential | 10% | Design flexibility |
| Regulatory watchlist status | 10% | EU CRMA / UK CMS alignment |
